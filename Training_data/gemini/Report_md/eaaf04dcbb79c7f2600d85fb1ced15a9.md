# **The Scroll of Ages: A Comprehensive Chronological History of Tamriel**

### **Introduction**

This report presents a detailed, analytical history of the continent of Tamriel, structured chronologically according to the in-universe system of Eras. The objective is to provide an exhaustive account of the major events, from the mythological genesis of reality to the political turmoil of the early Fourth Era, arranging the legends of *The Elder Scrolls* in the order they occurred.

A fundamental challenge in studying Tamrielic history is the nature of its sources. The lore is not presented as objective fact but through in-universe books, scrolls, and accounts that are frequently contradictory, colored by cultural bias, or framed as pure myth. Events like the "Dragon Break," a phenomenon where time itself becomes non-linear, and the chaotic nature of the primordial Dawn Era serve as metaphysical explanations for these inconsistencies. Therefore, this history must be approached not as a single, immutable truth, but as a complex tapestry woven from the diverse and often conflicting perspectives of the peoples who have shaped it. This report will navigate these complexities to present the most coherent timeline possible, exploring recurring themes such as the cyclical rise and fall of empires, the foundational conflict between the races of Man and Mer (Elves), the direct intervention of divine and Daedric entities, and the pivotal role of prophesied heroes in shaping the destiny of the world.

## **I. The Dawn Era: The Forging of Reality**

The Dawn Era is a period shrouded in myth, an "untime" before linear history began, where the very laws of reality were being forged. Its history is not a sequence of events but a collection of creation myths, or the Monomyth, that explain the origin of the cosmos and its inhabitants. The non-linear, paradoxical nature of this era is a core metaphysical principle of the universe, allowing for the simultaneous truth of multiple, often contradictory, creation stories. This establishes a world where belief and myth are not just interpretations of reality but are foundational components *of* reality itself.

### **The Monomyth: Anu, Padomay, and the et'Ada**

Before existence, there were two primordial forces: Anu, the embodiment of stasis and order, and Padomay, the embodiment of change and chaos. Their interplay created the Aurbis, the cosmic totality from which all things emerged. From this struggle, the original spirits, the et'Ada, came into being. Two of the most significant were Akatosh (known as Auri-El to the Elves), who first established the concept of time, and Lorkhan, a Padomaic spirit of limits and trickery.

The et'Ada would eventually be categorized by mortals based on their role in creation. Those who participated became the Aedra, an Aldmeri term meaning "our ancestors." Those who refused to take part, preserving their full power and agency, became known as the Daedra, or "not our ancestors". This distinction is not one of good versus evil but of creative investment. A third group, the Magna-Ge, initially agreed to creation but fled with its chief architect, the spirit Magnus, before they were fully bound to it. Their escape tore holes in the fabric of Oblivion, creating the sun (the largest tear, left by Magnus) and the stars, through which the magical energy of Aetherius leaks into the mortal world.

### **Lorkhan's Gambit and the Creation of Mundus**

The creation of the mortal plane, Mundus (of which the planet Nirn is the center), was Lorkhan's idea. He convinced or tricked the other et'Ada into investing their own power to create a new realm of limitations, mortality, and potential. The cultural interpretations of this act form a core conflict that echoes through the eras. Human mythologies generally view Lorkhan (often called Shor) as a heroic figure who gifted them existence and the opportunity for change in a static universe. In contrast, Elven mythologies see him as a malevolent trickster who severed them from their inherent divinity and trapped them in a mortal prison. This fundamental disagreement over the virtue of creation itself is the source of the ancient animosity between Man and Mer.

### **The Convention and the Stabilization of Time**

When the Aedra realized that creation was draining their power and rendering them mortal, they turned on Lorkhan. A divine war erupted, culminating in the Aedric "Convention" at the Adamantine Tower, the oldest known structure in Tamriel. There, they passed judgment on the trickster god. Lorkhan's heart was torn from his chest by the elven hero-god Trinimac and fired from a bow by Auri-El. Where it landed, the great volcano known as Red Mountain would later form. His body was sundered, and its remnants are said to have become the twin moons, Masser and Secunda.

With Lorkhan's divine spark removed, the remaining Aedra established the physical laws of Nirn, becoming the Ehlnofey, or "Earth Bones," the very framework of nature. This act stabilized reality, forcing the chaotic flow of time into a linear path and marking the end of the Dawn Era. History, as a recordable sequence of events, could now begin.

## **II. The Merethic Era: The Age of Myth and Migration**

The Merethic Era, or "Era of the Elves," is the first historical period with a linear timeline. However, its chronology is unique, established by early Nord scholars who counted the years backward from their first recorded historical event, the founding of the Camoran Dynasty in 1E 1. Thus, the era spans from ME 2500 to ME 1. This was a foundational age defined by the arrival of Tamriel's most prominent races and the rise of legendary, now-lost civilizations.

### **The First Footfalls: The Settlement of Tamriel**

During the Merethic Era, Tamriel was primarily inhabited by aboriginal "beastfolk," the ancestors of the Khajiit and Argonians. This changed with waves of migration from other continents.

* **The Aldmer:** The first Elves, known as the Aldmer, arrived from their lost, possibly mythical continent of Aldmeris. They established sophisticated and technologically advanced colonies along the coasts, driving the native beastfolk into the jungles, swamps, and wastelands of the interior.
* **The Atmorans:** The first humans to arrive in Tamriel were the Atmorans, who sailed from the northern continent of Atmora under the leadership of the legendary Ysgramor. They founded the city of Saarthal in what would become Skyrim, leading to the first contact between Man and Mer on the continent.
* **The Beastfolk:** The origins of the Khajiit and Argonians are distinct from those of Men and Mer. The Argonians, or Saxhleel, are believed to be descended directly from the Hist, a mysterious and sentient species of trees in their homeland of Black Marsh. Their souls are intrinsically linked to the Hist through its sap. The Khajiit have a unique creation myth involving the Daedric Prince Azura, who is said to have shaped them from a group of "forest people," giving them their many forms tied to the phases of the moons. This myth suggests a shared ancestry with the Bosmer (Wood Elves), who were also part of this shapeless folk before making a pact with the god Y'ffre.

### **The Lost Civilizations**

The Merethic Era saw the flourishing of several powerful civilizations that would vanish or be irrevocably transformed by the time of the games.

* **The Ayleids:** Known as the "Wild Elves," the Ayleids ruled central Tamriel from the White-Gold Tower. They were masters of Dawn Era magic and enslaved the native Nedic human tribes, building a sprawling empire across Cyrodiil.
* **The Dwemer:** Called "Dwarves" by men, the Dwemer were a reclusive and highly logical elven clan who rejected the gods and devoted themselves to science, engineering, and alchemy. They built vast, technologically advanced underground cities in the mountains separating Skyrim and Morrowind.
* **The Snow Elves (Falmer):** Before the arrival of humans, Skyrim was the domain of the Snow Elves. A highly advanced and prosperous civilization, they were driven to the brink of extinction by the Nords. The survivors sought refuge with the Dwemer, who tricked them, blinded them, and enslaved them for generations, twisting them into the monstrous, degenerate Falmer encountered in subterranean ruins.

### **Foundational Conflicts**

Two major conflicts of this era set the stage for the racial and political dynamics of all subsequent history.

* **The Night of Tears:** The relationship between the Atmoran settlers and the Snow Elves in Skyrim turned hostile when the Atmorans discovered a powerful artifact, the Eye of Magnus, in their city of Saarthal. Fearing its power, the Elves attacked and sacked the city in an event known as the Night of Tears. Ysgramor and his two sons were among the few survivors who fled back to Atmora. He soon returned with his legendary Five Hundred Companions, an army that waged a genocidal campaign against the Snow Elves, ultimately conquering Skyrim for humanity and laying the foundation for the first human empire in Tamriel.
* **The Dragon War:** The Nords of this era were ruled by a tyrannical dragon cult, led by powerful Dragon Priests who served their draconic masters. A rebellion erupted, and though the Nords were initially outmatched, they received aid from a few dragons, most notably Paarthurnax. He taught them how to wield the Thu'um, or the Voice, a form of magic using the dragons' own language. With this new power, the Nords overthrew the dragons and their priests, ending their reign of terror. Alduin, the first-born of Akatosh and leader of the dragons, was not killed but cast forward in time by an Elder Scroll, a defeat that would have dire consequences thousands of years later.

## **III. The First Era: The Rise of Men and Empires (1E 1 - 1E 2920)**

Lasting 2,920 years, the First Era was a period of epic upheaval that decisively shifted the balance of power on the continent from Mer to Man. It was an age defined not just by the rise and fall of empires, but by profound transformations that altered the very nature of races and gods.

### **The Alessian Revolt (c. 1E 242)**

The era's first great turning point was the rebellion of the enslaved Nedic peoples of Cyrodiil against their Ayleid masters. Led by the "slave-queen" Alessia, the rebellion was bolstered by two crucial allies: the armies of Skyrim to the north and the demigod Pelinal Whitestrake. Pelinal was a figure of immense power and seemingly divine origin, possessed by a furious, unyielding hatred for all elves, and his prowess in battle was instrumental in the Ayleids' annihilation. After conquering the White-Gold Tower, Alessia established the first Cyrodilic Empire. In a masterful political move to unify her religiously divided followers, she created the pantheon of the Eight Divines, a syncretic faith that blended Nordic and Aldmeri deities to appease both her Nedic subjects and her Nordic allies.

### **The War of the First Council (c. 1E 700)**

In the northeastern land of Resdayn (modern Morrowind), a fragile peace between two elven peoples, the Daedra-worshipping Chimer and the secular Dwemer, shattered into war. The conflict was sparked by the Dwemer's ultimate act of hubris: their chief tonal architect, Kagrenac, had discovered the still-beating Heart of Lorkhan beneath Red Mountain and was building a mechanical god, the Numidium, to be powered by it. The Chimer, led by their hero Indoril Nerevar, viewed this as a profound blasphemy. The resulting War of the First Council culminated in the Battle of Red Mountain, a cataclysm that had two world-altering consequences. First, in the midst of the battle, the entire Dwemer race vanished from Nirn in an instant, leaving behind only their ghost-haunted ruins and still-functioning automatons. The cause remains one of history's greatest mysteries. Second, Nerevar's closest advisors—his wife Almalexia and his generals Sotha Sil and Vivec—betrayed him. They seized Kagrenac's tools, used them on the Heart of Lorkhan to steal its divine power, and made themselves the living gods of the Tribunal. For this betrayal, the Daedric Prince Azura cursed them and their people, transforming the golden-skinned Chimer into the grey-skinned, red-eyed Dunmer.

### **The Ra Gada and the Sinking of Yokuda (c. 1E 808)**

To the west, the continent of Yokuda suffered a cataclysm that sank most of it beneath the sea. Survivors fled east across the ocean to Tamriel. The first and largest of these migrations was the "Ra Gada" or "warrior wave," a massive fleet of Yokudan warriors who violently conquered the land that would become Hammerfell, slaughtering or displacing its native populations. These warriors became known as the Redguards. Their unique culture and martial traditions, along with fringe theories that Yokuda was not merely a lost continent but a previous "kalpa" or world-cycle, set them apart from the other human races of Tamriel.

### **The Reman Dynasty and the Akaviri Invasion (c. 1E 2703)**

Near the end of the era, Tamriel faced an invasion from the eastern continent of Akavir by the serpentine Tsaesci. The disparate human kingdoms of Cyrodiil united under a Colovian warlord, Reman Cyrodiil, who defeated the invaders at Pale Pass. Reman forged the Second Empire of Men and incorporated the surrendered Akaviri warriors into his army as the Dragonguard, an elite guard that would eventually become the Blades. The First Era concluded with the assassination of the last ruler of this dynasty, Reman III, by the Morag Tong, leaving the Empire under the stewardship of his Akaviri Potentate, Versidue-Shaie.

## **IV. The Second Era: The Interregnum and the Coming of Talos (2E 1 - 2E 896)**

The Second Era, lasting 896 years, was defined by a prolonged period of chaos that ultimately created the conditions for an unprecedented unification. The extreme fragmentation and constant warfare of this age forged a power vacuum and a continental desperation for stability, which only a figure of singular ambition and ruthlessness could fill.

### **The Interregnum: An Age of Chaos (c. 2E 430 onwards)**

The era began under the rule of the Akaviri Potentates, who governed the Empire after the end of the Reman line. This period of stability ended abruptly in 2E 430 when Potentate Savirien-Chorak and all his heirs were assassinated by the Dark Brotherhood. With no emperor and no potentate, the Second Empire collapsed. Tamriel was plunged into a dark age of nearly 450 years known as the Interregnum. Without a central authority, the continent fractured into warring states, with petty kings, warlords, and would-be emperors fighting for scraps of power. Despite the chaos, this period saw the formation of continent-spanning institutions like the Mages Guild and the Fighters Guild, founded to bring a semblance of order and regulation to their respective trades.

### **The Three Banners War and the Planemeld (c. 2E 582)**

The chaos of the Interregnum reached its zenith in the mid-sixth century with the Three Banners War, the central conflict of *The Elder Scrolls Online*. With the Ruby Throne of Cyrodiil empty, three powerful alliances formed to claim it: the Daggerfall Covenant (Bretons, Redguards, and Orcs), the Ebonheart Pact (Nords, Dunmer, and Argonians), and the first Aldmeri Dominion (Altmer, Bosmer, and Khajiit). As these factions waged a bloody war across Tamriel, the Daedric Prince of Domination, Molag Bal, launched his own invasion. In an event known as the Planemeld, he used massive constructs called Dark Anchors to begin pulling Nirn into his hellish realm of Coldharbour. The invasion was ultimately thwarted by a soulless hero known as the Vestige, with the aid of the other Daedric Princes and the last of the Reman-era Dragonguard.

### **The Tiber Wars and the Unification of Tamriel (c. 2E 854-896)**

Out of the ashes of the Interregnum rose Hjalti Early-Beard, a Colovian warlord who would become known to history as General Talos, and later, Tiber Septim. A military and political genius, he began a sweeping campaign to unite Tamriel under his rule. His conquest was swift and brutal, crushing all resistance. When faced with the formidable power of the second Aldmeri Dominion in the Summerset Isles, Septim unleashed the Numidium, the long-dormant brass god of the Dwemer, which he had reassembled and powered with a soul gem known as the Mantella. The sheer destructive power of the Numidium forced the elves' surrender. In 2E 896, having conquered the entire continent, Tiber Septim declared the beginning of a new age, founding the Third Empire and the Septim Dynasty that would rule it for over four centuries.

## **V. The Third Era: The Glory and Collapse of the Septim Dynasty (3E 1 - 3E 433)**

The Third Era, lasting 433 years, is synonymous with the reign of the Septim Dynasty and is the setting for most of the mainline *Elder Scrolls* games. It was an age of relative peace and prosperity under a unified Empire, punctuated by dynastic struggles and the actions of prophesied heroes who repeatedly saved Tamriel from disaster.

### **The Septim Dynasty**

Tiber Septim's reign was followed by a long and complex line of successors. The dynasty saw periods of great strength, such as the prosperous rule of Empress Kintyra I, and periods of devastating internal conflict, most notably the War of the Red Diamond (3E 120-127), a brutal civil war instigated by the "Wolf Queen" Potema of Solitude against her own family. It also saw periods of bold ambition, like the reign of Uriel V, who launched a massive but ultimately disastrous invasion of the continent of Akavir, where he was killed in battle. Despite these challenges, the Septim line endured, maintaining the unity Tiber Septim had forged.

### **The Age of Heroes: The Mainline Games**

The latter half of the era was defined by a series of crises that were resolved by legendary individuals.

* **The Imperial Simulacrum (3E 389-399):** Emperor Uriel Septim VII was betrayed by his Imperial Battlemage, Jagar Tharn. Tharn imprisoned the emperor in a realm of Oblivion and used powerful illusion magic to assume his identity, ruling the Empire for ten years. This decade of misrule and conflict, known as the Imperial Simulacrum, was ended when a lone hero, the Eternal Champion, reassembled the shattered Staff of Chaos and defeated the usurper, freeing the true emperor. This is the story of *The Elder Scrolls: Arena*.
* **The Warp in the West (3E 417):** The events of *The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall* centered on the political intrigue of the Iliac Bay kingdoms and the struggle to control the Numidium. The crisis was resolved when an agent of the Emperor activated the brass god. The immense power of the Numidium caused a "Dragon Break," an event where time fractured and linear causality broke down. For a brief period, multiple, mutually exclusive outcomes occurred simultaneously, all becoming true. When time reconsolidated, the political landscape of High Rock and Hammerfell had been violently simplified, with dozens of petty kingdoms consolidated into a few regional powers. This event is known as the Warp in the West or, more optimistically, the Miracle of Peace.
* **The Nerevarine Prophecy (3E 427):** The events of *The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind* saw the fulfillment of an ancient Dunmer prophecy. The player character, a prisoner sent to Vvardenfell, was revealed to be the reincarnation of the ancient hero Indoril Nerevar. The Nerevarine destroyed the Heart of Lorkhan, the divine power source that the demigod Dagoth Ur and the Tribunal gods had used for millennia. This act killed Dagoth Ur, ended the divine rule of the Tribunal, and cured Morrowind of the soul-twisting Blight disease.

### **The Oblivion Crisis (3E 433)**

The Third Era ended in a continent-wide cataclysm. A Daedric cult known as the Mythic Dawn, acting on the orders of the Daedric Prince of Destruction, Mehrunes Dagon, assassinated Emperor Uriel Septim VII and all his known heirs. The death of the last Dragonborn emperors broke the ancient covenant that protected Nirn from the forces of Oblivion. Oblivion Gates opened across Tamriel, unleashing Daedric armies that ravaged the land. The crisis was ended only when the last undiscovered heir, a priest named Martin Septim, sacrificed himself by shattering the Amulet of Kings. This act merged his soul with the divine essence of Akatosh, creating a fiery avatar of the Dragon God that banished Mehrunes Dagon from the mortal plane forever. Martin's sacrifice saved the world but extinguished the Septim bloodline, ending the Third Era and leaving the Empire leaderless and broken.

## **VI. The Fourth Era: The Waning of the Empire (4E 1 - 4E 201+)**

The Fourth Era began with an Empire in shambles, its divine covenant broken and its provinces in disarray. This power vacuum allowed for the rise of a new and aggressive elven superpower, the Aldmeri Dominion, leading to a continent-spanning conflict that was not merely territorial, but ideological. The core of this conflict is a metaphysical struggle over the nature of divinity and humanity's place in the world.

### **The Aftermath of Crisis**

The era opened on a leaderless Empire, as High Chancellor Ocato, who had tried to hold the government together as Potentate, was assassinated in 4E 10. This triggered the Stormcrown Interregnum, a period of civil war in Cyrodiil. The provinces suffered greatly. In 4E 5, the "Red Year" occurred: the Ministry of Truth, a meteor that had been magically suspended over Vivec City in Morrowind for eras, finally fell. The impact triggered a catastrophic eruption of Red Mountain that devastated the island of Vvardenfell and much of the Dunmer homeland. In the chaos, the Argonians of Black Marsh invaded the crippled province, and the High King of Skyrim granted the island of Solstheim to the Dunmer refugees.

### **The Rise of the Third Aldmeri Dominion**

While the Empire crumbled, the Thalmor, a radical elven supremacist faction, seized power in the Summerset Isles. They claimed to have saved the isles during the Oblivion Crisis, using this narrative to justify their coup. They re-established the Aldmeri Dominion and, in 4E 29, overthrew the government of Valenwood, annexing the province. Their influence grew further after the "Void Nights" (4E 98-100), a two-year period where Nirn's moons vanished. The Thalmor claimed credit for their return, a feat that won them the allegiance of the Khajiit of Elsweyr, whose culture is deeply tied to the moons.

### **The Great War (4E 171-175)**

After decades of consolidation, the Dominion was ready to challenge the weakened Empire, now ruled by the Mede Dynasty. In 4E 171, a Thalmor ambassador delivered an ultimatum to Emperor Titus Mede II, demanding massive territorial concessions, the disbandment of the Blades, and, most significantly, the outlawing of Talos worship. The Emperor refused, and the Great War began. The Dominion launched a two-pronged invasion, attacking Hammerfell and Cyrodiil simultaneously. The war raged for four years, culminating in the brutal Sack of the Imperial City in 4E 174. However, in a brilliant strategic maneuver known as the Battle of the Red Ring, Titus Mede II lured the Dominion army into a trap and annihilated it, retaking the capital.

### **The White-Gold Concordat and its Consequences**

Despite the stunning victory, the Imperial legions were exhausted and decimated. Believing he could not sustain the war, Titus Mede II signed the White-Gold Concordat, a peace treaty that largely acceded to the Thalmor's original demands. The consequences were catastrophic for Imperial unity.

* **The Ban on Talos Worship:** For the Nords of Skyrim, Talos—the ascended human Emperor Tiber Septim—is not just a hero but a god. Forcing them to abandon his worship was seen as a profound betrayal and an unforgivable capitulation. This single clause was the primary catalyst for the Stormcloak Rebellion. The Thalmor's insistence on this point reveals their true objective: it is not merely about land, but about de-legitimizing humanity's greatest achievement—a mortal ascending to godhood. By forcing the Empire to police the faith of its own citizens, the Thalmor turned the Concordat into a weapon of ideological warfare.
* **The Cession of Hammerfell:** The treaty demanded that a large portion of southern Hammerfell be ceded to the Dominion. The Redguards refused to yield their land and were subsequently renounced as an Imperial province by the Emperor to preserve the treaty. In a remarkable display of martial prowess, the Redguards fought the Dominion to a standstill alone, eventually forcing the elven armies to withdraw in 4E 180. This created a new, independent, and deeply embittered Redguard nation.

### **The Dragon Crisis and Stormcloak Rebellion (4E 201)**

These simmering tensions exploded in 4E 201, the year in which the events of *The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim* take place. Ulfric Stormcloak, Jarl of Windhelm and a veteran of the Great War, challenged and killed the High King of Skyrim, sparking a full-blown civil war between his secessionist Stormcloaks and the Imperial Legion. As Skyrim tore itself apart, Alduin the World-Eater, the dragon defeated in the Merethic Era, returned as prophesied to devour the world. His re-emergence resurrected the long-dead dragons and awakened the last Dragonborn, a hero destined to stop him.

## **Conclusion: The Unwritten Scroll**

As of the year 4E 201, Tamriel stands on a knife's edge. The Empire of the Medes is a shadow of its former glory, its legions depleted, its treasury drained, and its authority challenged from within and without. The Aldmeri Dominion is the continent's undisputed superpower, its armies poised for a second war to finish what the first began, its long-term goals of undoing creation itself veiled in secrecy. Hammerfell stands alone, a proud and independent nation forged in defiance. Morrowind is a land of ash and refugees. Black Marsh is isolationist and hostile.

In Skyrim, the civil war rages, a proxy conflict in the larger ideological struggle between the Empire and the Dominion. The outcome of this war, combined with the re-emergence of the dragons and the actions of the last Dragonborn, will undoubtedly shape the future of the continent. The central conflict remains unresolved: the inevitable second Great War looms, a final struggle that will determine whether the future of Tamriel belongs to the diverse but fractured races of Men or the unified and supremacist vision of the Thalmor. The scroll of history is still being written, and its next chapter promises to be one of fire and blood.
